About Me


I am a collaborative and student-centered educator and student affairs professional with experience supporting students through transitions, supervising undergraduate workers, and building engagement. My work is guided by strong people-centered values — responsibility, empathy, and flexibility — which shape how I design programs, solve challenges, and create spaces where students can grow, succeed, and feel a sense of belonging. I am especially passionate about restorative practices, mentorship, and fostering community within higher education environments.

My journey into Higher Education Administration began not with a desire for authority, but with a commitment to students. While working as a teacher with the Ghana Education Service and later as a transition coach with CAMFED Ghana, I saw firsthand that many students needed more than academic instruction — they needed guidance, mentoring, and structured support to navigate their educational paths. These experiences motivated me to pursue graduate study and move from informal support to more informed, systems-level leadership.

Through my graduate assistantship with Student Engagement & Transition Programs (STEP), internship experiences, and involvement in campus committees — including the William & Mary Hazing Prevention Committee, Office of Conflict Resolution & Education, and partnerships with the Virginia Center for Restorative Justice — I have developed a stronger understanding of leadership, student development, and institutional responsibility.
